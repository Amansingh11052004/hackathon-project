Discover Varanasi

About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Banaras, is a city on the banks of the Ganges in Uttar Pradesh, India. It's one of the most sacred cities in Hinduism and has a rich history stretching back thousands of years.

Famous Locations:

  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple: One of the most important temples dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Dasaswamedh Ghat: A lively and sacred ghat where daily Ganga Aarti is performed.
  • Sarnath: The place where Gautama Buddha first taught the Dharma.
  • Manikarnika Ghat: Known for its historical significance as a cremation ghat.

    • The Divinity of Varanasi

    For Hindus, Varanasi represents a portal to salvation. Thousands of devotees flock to the city every day, seeking blessings and spiritual cleansing in the sacred waters of the Ganges. One of the key beliefs is that dying in Varanasi and being cremated along the banks of the Ganges breaks the cycle of rebirth and grants moksha (liberation of the soul). The city's ghats, especially Manikarnika Ghat and Harishchandra Ghat, are famed for cremation rituals, which are said to be the most sacred in Hinduism.

    Tourism in Varanasi

    Tourism in Varanasi is centered around its rich culture, ancient temples, and ghats. One of the most famous landmarks is the Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and a major pilgrimage site for Hindus. Other must-visit temples include the Durga Temple and the Sankat Mochan Temple.

    The Ganga Aarti, held every evening at the Dasaswamedh Ghat, is a mesmerizing ceremony of light, sound, and devotion. Hundreds of people gather along the ghats to witness the priests perform this sacred ritual, where oil lamps are lit and waved to honor the river goddess Ganga. The vibrant scene, coupled with the sunset, offers an unforgettable experience for tourists and devotees alike.

    Exploring the Ghats

    Varanasi is home to over 80 ghats, each with its own significance. The ghats are riverfront steps leading to the Ganges, where people come to bathe, pray, meditate, and perform religious rituals. Assi Ghat, Dashashwamedh Ghat, and Chet Singh Ghat are among the most popular. Visitors can take boat rides along the Ganges, offering panoramic views of the city's spiritual and historic landscape.

    A Hub for Culture and Heritage

    Apart from its religious significance, Varanasi is also known as a center of Indian art, music, and education. The city has been the birthplace of some of India's greatest scholars, musicians, and poets. The Banaras Hindu University (BHU), established in 1916, is one of the largest residential universities in Asia and an esteemed center of learning.

    Varanasi is also famous for its silk weaving, producing some of the finest Banarasi silk saris in the world. The narrow, bustling streets of the city are filled with local artisans, selling these exquisite handwoven fabrics.

    Sarnath: The Buddhist Connection

    Just a short drive from Varanasi lies Sarnath, a significant Buddhist pilgrimage site. It is here that Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. The Dhamek Stupa and the Chaukhandi Stupa are prominent landmarks in Sarnath, attracting visitors from all over the world who come to meditate and connect with the teachings of Buddha.
